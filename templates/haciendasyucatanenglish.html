There was a time when Yucatán had more contact with Europe and the Caribbean than with the rest of the country because the state produced abundantly what they then called "green gold": henequen. This plant extracted robust fibers to make textiles, ropes, and navigation cords. The best testimony of those prosperous years is the haciendas of Yucatán, true palaces established by the owners of the plantations. And although technological innovations and revolutions ended that period of wealth, leading to the abandonment of many of these haciendas, many of them have been reborn in the 21st century to offer an experience of absolute luxury.
