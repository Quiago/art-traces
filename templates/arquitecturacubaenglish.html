For architecture enthusiasts, visiting Havana will be an intense experience. Defined by Carpentier as the "City of style without styles," Havana offers visitors the charm and mysteries of a legendary city. Walking its streets and visiting different neighborhoods is similar to a journey through time, where you can see all the architectural trends that have shaped Havana's profile and have established our city as one of the most important in Latin America and the Caribbean. Founded in 1519, the City of Havana was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1982. The Old City and its system of fortifications are a classic example of colonial architecture, along with private residences, luxurious palaces, and wonderful squares from the 16th and 17th centuries. The Malecón, our balcony to the Caribbean Sea, is the protagonist of change, where old buildings give way to the modern city, where eclecticism, modernism, art nouveau, art deco, and contemporary trends share the shade of trees on wide avenues and parks. Cerro, El Vedado, Santo Suárez, or Miramar are some of the neighborhoods where typical architecture will provide us with a particular perspective on Cuban history and help us understand how changes in the socio-cultural context of the island contributed to shaping the current identity of the Cuban capital.
