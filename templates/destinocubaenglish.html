About Cuba

Cuba is undoubtedly one of the top choices when planning a trip to the Caribbean. This is not only due to its stunning beaches, wonderful year-round tropical climate, and curiosity as one of the last socialist countries in the world but above all, for the recognized warmth of its people, delicious cuisine, and especially for the high level of its artistic proposals, making it a prime destination for cultural tourism enthusiasts. A visit to Cuba must include its capital, Havana, founded in 1519 as the most important city in the New World, and whose historic center was declared a World Heritage Site in 1982. The original urban structure of Old Havana remains intact. Narrow cobblestone streets lead to beautiful squares, churches, fortresses, and beautiful palaces among more than 900 buildings considered jewels of colonial, baroque, neoclassical, or art deco architecture that recall the greatness of a city considered until the early 20th century as "The Key to the Gulf." Always bathed by the sea and surrounded by its impressive Malecón, exploring Havana is to traverse the history of architecture, from colonial to modern Havana, with its residential neighborhoods, romantic hotels, and impressive museums and theaters that earned it the reputation of being one of the three most important cities in America in the first half of the 20th century.
