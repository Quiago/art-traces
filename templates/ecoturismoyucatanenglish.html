Yucatán is a privilege, as the jungle that practically surrounds all the towns and cities holds caves, cenotes, and a large variety of flora and fauna whose sounds will make you forget the noise of big cities. You can peacefully swim in a cenote surrounded by lush vegetation or admire the beautiful pink flamingos of Celestún on the west coast of Yucatán. Yucatán's ecological centers offer activities such as rappelling, kayaking, hiking, cenote swimming, biking, and nature observation. Many of these alternative tourism centers are managed by descendants of the Mayans, who, leveraging the natural beauty of their towns or communities, have created this way of connecting with nature. Enjoy it, but above all, respect the environment so that others dare to discover the mysteries that the land holds.
